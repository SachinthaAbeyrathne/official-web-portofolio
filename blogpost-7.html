"Exploring the Enchanting
Beauty of Wales: A Memorable Journey"

POSTED ON JUL 28,2023

As an International Student, I recently had the incredible opportunity to embark on a soul-stirring trip to Wales, UK. Armed with my sense of curiosity and a National Express coach ticket, I journeyed from London Victoria coach station to Sophia Gardens Coach Station in Cardiff. Over the course of several days, I delved into the mesmerizing landscapes, rich history, and captivating culture that make Wales a hidden gem. Join me as I recount the highlights of this unforgettable journey.

"Venturing into the heart of Wales as an international student is a journey of discovery that transcends borders and unearths the treasures of a captivating land. From its lush landscapes to its rich cultural heritage, every moment in Wales is an invitation to broaden your horizons, embrace diversity, and create cherished memories that will resonate with you forever."

Location: The Royal Pier Arcade, Aberystwyth, Wales UK


Cardiff: A Vibrant Metropolis with a Seaside Twist

My first destination was Cardiff, the dynamic capital city of Wales. I found myself instantly drawn to the charm of Cardiff Bay, where modern architecture harmoniously blends with the picturesque waterfront. The Wales Millennium Centre, a cultural hub that showcases various performances and events, left me in awe with its stunning design and cultural significance.

A Glimpse into the Heart of Welsh Heritage

A short journey from Cardiff led me to Llantrisant, home to the Royal Mint. Stepping into this historic institution, I immersed myself in the art of coin-making and learned about the integral role the Royal Mint plays in preserving the nation's heritage. The experience provided me with a deep appreciation for the artistry and craftsmanship behind the coins we use every day.

Finding Peace at Skanda Vale

Seeking a spiritual escape, I traveled to Carmarthenshire and discovered Skanda Vale. This serene multi-faith sanctuary nestled amidst lush woodlands captivated me with its tranquil atmosphere. Participating in peaceful Hindu rituals and witnessing the love and unity among the devotees left me with a sense of inner peace and harmony.

Aberystwyth: A Seaside Town Steeped in History

Continuing my journey, I arrived in Aberystwyth, a charming seaside town. The Royal Pier Arcade, a historic Victorian arcade, welcomed me with its nostalgic charm. As I strolled along the pier and took in the breathtaking views of the sea, I couldn't help but appreciate the coastal beauty that Wales has to offer.

A Cozy Haven at Cefn Bryn Cottage

Throughout my Welsh adventure, Cefn Bryn Cottage in Cwmann, Lampeter, served as my cozy home away from home. Nestled amidst rolling hills and picturesque countryside, this quaint cottage offered warm hospitality and an authentic taste of rural Welsh life. Each night, I was lulled to sleep by the soothing sounds of nature, leaving me refreshed and ready for another day of exploration.

My recent trip to Wales, UK, was a journey that touched my soul and left me with cherished memories to last a lifetime. From the vibrant metropolis of Cardiff to the tranquil sanctuaries and historic sites, Wales offered a perfect blend of adventure and tranquility. Each place visited, from Cardiff Bay and the Wales Millennium Centre to the Royal Mint Llantrisant, Skanda Vale Carmarthenshire, and Royal Pier Arcade Aberystwyth, revealed a unique aspect of Wales' rich heritage and enchanting beauty. As I boarded the National Express coach to return to London, I couldn't help but feel a sense of gratitude for the experiences and connections made during my time in Wales. It was more than just a trip; it was an exploration of the heart and soul of a country that has so much to offer to those willing to venture off the beaten path.

If you're planning a trip to Cardiff from London, here are some important tips to ensure a smooth and enjoyable journey:


1. Plan Your Itinerary: Cardiff offers a wide range of attractions and activities, so plan your itinerary in advance to make the most of your time. Research the places you want to visit and check their opening hours and any entry requirements.

2. Choose Your Mode of Transport: Decide on your preferred mode of transport. Whether it's a train, coach, or car, book your tickets or make arrangements ahead of time to secure the best options and prices.

3. Travel Time: Consider the travel time from London to Cardiff and plan your departure and return accordingly. Be mindful of potential delays, especially during peak travel seasons.

4. Accommodation: Book your accommodation in advance to ensure you have a comfortable place to stay. Cardiff offers a range of options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and cozy guesthouses.

5. Pack Appropriately: Check the weather forecast for Cardiff during your travel dates and pack accordingly. Don't forget essentials like comfortable walking shoes, an umbrella, and any necessary travel documents.

6. Explore Cardiff Bay: Don't miss the vibrant Cardiff Bay area. Explore the waterfront, enjoy the beautiful views, and indulge in delicious food and drinks at the numerous restaurants and cafes.

7. Visit Landmarks: Plan a visit to iconic landmarks like Cardiff Castle, the Wales Millennium Centre, and the Principality Stadium. These attractions offer a glimpse into Cardiff's rich history and cultural heritage.

8. Public Transport: Cardiff has an efficient public transport system. Consider getting an Oyster card or a local travel pass for buses and trains to easily navigate the city.

9. Safety: Prioritize safety during your trip. Be aware of your surroundings, keep your belongings secure, and follow any local safety guidelines.

10. Respect Local Customs: Familiarize yourself with local customs and traditions to show respect for Cardiff's culture and people.

11. Try Local Cuisine: Don't miss the opportunity to sample Welsh cuisine, such as Welsh cakes, cawl (traditional soup), and bara brith (fruit loaf).

By following these tips, you'll be well-prepared to embark on a memorable and enjoyable trip to Cardiff from London. Cardiff's vibrant atmosphere, historical landmarks, and warm hospitality are sure to leave you with lasting memories of your Welsh adventure.
